Practical information for living in Preston

Tap an option below to read detailed guidance. This is not official advice, but a simple community guide to help Nepali members.

Finding jobs in Preston

  • Online job sites: Use UK job websites (Indeed, Reed, Totaljobs, LinkedIn). Search with keywords like Preston, warehouse, care assistant, kitchen staff, etc.
  • CV & cover letter: Prepare an English CV with UK phone number, address, work history and references. Keep it simple and clear.
  • Walk-in applications: Some restaurants, shops and warehouses accept CVs directly. Always be polite and ask if they are hiring.
  • Agencies: Recruitment agencies can help with warehouse, care and hospitality jobs. Members can share trusted agencies in the group.
  • Group help: In the WhatsApp group, members can share: job links, referrals, interview tips and warnings about bad employers.

Always check that offers are genuine. Never pay money just to apply for a job.

Medical system & GP registration (NHS)

  • Register with a GP: Find a local GP (doctor‚Äôs clinic) near your address. Every person should be registered with one GP practice.
  • Documents usually needed: ID (passport/BRP), proof of address (tenancy agreement, bill or bank letter). Some GPs accept new patients online.
  • Appointments: For non-emergency issues, call your GP or use their website to book a routine appointment or telephone consultation.
  • Urgent help: For urgent but not life-threatening issues, call NHS 111 or use 111 online. For emergencies (life in danger) call 999.
  • Prescriptions: After doctor consultation, you may receive a prescription to collect medicine from a pharmacy (charges may apply).

Members can share which GP surgeries are accepting new patients and their experience with local hospitals, dentists and pharmacies.

Opening a bank account

  • Choose a bank: Common options include high street banks (Barclays, NatWest, Santander, HSBC) and app-based banks (Monzo, Starling).
  • Basic documents: Usually need ID (passport/BRP) and proof of address (tenancy agreement, bill, employer or university letter).
  • How to apply: Many banks allow account opening through their mobile app. Others require an appointment at a local branch.
  • Check fees: Look at card type, international transfer costs, and if the account is free or has monthly charges.
  • Safety: Never share your PIN or one-time codes in WhatsApp or with strangers. Bank staff will never ask for full passwords by phone.

Buying a SIM card & mobile plan

  • Main networks: EE, O2, Vodafone, Three and others. Supermarkets often sell SIMs (Tesco, ASDA, Aldi etc).
  • Pay-as-you-go vs contract: Pay-as-you-go is flexible with no long contract. Monthly contract gives more data but may require credit check.
  • What to check: Coverage in your area, data allowance, minutes for UK calls and international call options to Nepal.
  • Registration: Some SIMs require an app or website registration. Keep your UK address and ID ready.
  • Community help: Members can share which networks work best in different parts of Preston and which bundles are good value.

Other important information for new arrivals

  • National Insurance Number: Needed for most jobs. Apply online through the UK government website; keep your documents ready.
  • Emergency numbers: 999 for emergency, 101 for non-emergency police, 111 for medical advice. Many 0800 numbers are free from mobiles.
  • Documents safety: Keep passport, BRP and contracts in a safe place. Take photos or scans for backup.
  • Scam awareness: Be careful with calls or messages asking for money or personal information. If unsure, ask the group before acting.
  • Language support: Many organisations offer translation help. Nepali friends in the group can also help explain things in simple language.
Visa & immigration (summary)

Student, Graduate (PSW) & ILR ‚Äì community overview (2025)

Simple explanation for Nepali students and workers in Preston, based on information from GOV.UK and official guidance. Rules can change ‚Äì always check the latest details on GOV.UK before applying. This is not legal advice.

Last checked: December 2025.

Student visa ‚Äì key points

  • Eligibility: Offer and CAS from a licensed college/university, enough money for tuition and living costs, and English language requirements. (See GOV.UK ‚ÄúStudent visa‚Äù page.)
  • Work: Many degree-level students can work part-time in term time and more hours in holidays ‚Äì check the work conditions printed on your BRP or eVisa.
  • Dependants: Only some students can bring a partner/children (for example certain postgraduate courses or government-sponsored students). Check ‚ÄúYour partner and children‚Äù section on GOV.UK or trusted sites such as UKCISA.
  • Stay length: Usually course length plus some extra time before/after the course ‚Äì details are set by the immigration rules.
üîó GOV.UK ‚Äì Student visa

Graduate visa (post-study / PSW)

  • What it is: Lets you stay in the UK after successfully completing an eligible UK course, to work or look for work. It is an unsponsored route (no job offer needed).
  • Length of stay: Currently usually 2 years, or 3 years if you completed a PhD or other doctoral qualification. For applications on or after 1 January 2027, most graduates will normally get 18 months instead of 2 years ‚Äì check GOV.UK for the exact rule at the time you apply.
  • Work rules: You can work at most skill levels and change jobs freely, but there are some limits (for example you cannot work as a professional sportsperson).
  • Next step: The Graduate visa cannot be extended ‚Äì to stay longer you usually move to another route (for example Skilled Worker) and later may qualify for ILR.
üîó GOV.UK ‚Äì Graduate visa

Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR) / settlement

  • What ILR is: ILR (settlement) gives you the right to live and work in the UK without time limit. It is often a step towards British citizenship.
  • Common routes: Many people qualify after a period on work or family visas (often 5 years), or after long residence (for example 10 years of lawful stay). Different visas have different rules.
  • Continuous residence: The rules look at ‚Äúcontinuous‚Äù lawful stay and can limit how long you can be outside the UK in each year.
  • Earned settlement consultation: In 2025 the UK government started consulting on an ‚Äúearned settlement‚Äù system which could change ILR rules from around 2026 onwards. It may lengthen some routes (for example to 10 years) and offer quicker routes for some people. Until rules change, the existing ILR rules still apply.

üëâ Always read the latest ILR pages on GOV.UK or speak to a qualified immigration adviser or solicitor before making big decisions.

Part-time work, cost of living & first week in Preston

Simple tools and guides to help new students and workers stay within visa rules and manage money. Amounts are examples only ‚Äì everyone‚Äôs costs are different.

Part-time hours & income (example calculator)

Enter your hours and hourly rate to see an example weekly income.

Cost of living ‚Äì example breakdown (Preston)

  • Room in shared house: Approx. range for many students is lower than some big UK cities. Check local adverts and never pay large cash without a receipt.
  • Food: Cooking at home (Asian / Nepali groceries) is normally much cheaper than eating out often.
  • Transport: Local bus passes, student discounts and railcards can save money on regular trips.
  • Other costs: Phone, internet, study materials, clothes, occasional visits to Nepal.

These are only rough ideas, not fixed prices. Members can share up-to-date examples in the WhatsApp group.

First week in Preston ‚Äì simple checklist

  • Buy a UK SIM card and set up mobile banking / WhatsApp.
  • Register with your university/college and collect student ID.
  • Check your tenancy agreement, bills and house rules.
  • Register with a GP and find the nearest pharmacy.
  • Learn main bus routes / train station and download travel apps.
  • Save emergency numbers and important contacts.
  • Join the NIP WhatsApp group and introduce yourself politely.

Housing checklist ‚Äì before you move in

  • Read the tenancy agreement carefully (rent amount, deposit, notice period, who pays bills).
  • Check if bills are included (gas, electricity, water, council tax, internet).
  • Take photos or videos of the room and property on the first day as evidence of condition.
  • Avoid paying large amounts of cash without a written receipt or bank transfer proof.
  • Ask about house rules (guests, noise, cleaning rota, smoking, pets).
  • If something does not feel right or safe, ask trusted friends or the group before agreeing.

About Preston and surrounding cities

Preston ‚Äì quick overview

Preston is a city in Lancashire, North West England. It has a mix of students, families and workers from many countries, including a growing Nepali community.

  • Home to the University of Central Lancashire (UCLan).
  • Good transport links by train and bus to Manchester, Blackpool and beyond.
  • Shops in the city centre, Fishergate and Deepdale Retail Park.
  • Parks and open spaces such as Avenham Park and Moor Park.

Frequently asked questions

Who can join the Nepalese in Preston (NIP) WhatsApp group?

Mainly Nepalese people living, working or studying in Preston and nearby areas. Friends of the Nepali community who want to support respectfully are also welcome.

Can I promote my business or YouTube/TikTok channel?

Only if it is clearly useful for the community and not spammy. Repeated promotion, unrelated links or clickbait are not allowed. Ask the admin first if unsure.
